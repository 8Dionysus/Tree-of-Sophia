# QUESTBOOK integration — Tree-of-Sophia

## Purpose

This note shows how `QUESTBOOK.md` can exist inside `Tree-of-Sophia` without flattening philosophical meaning into generic AoA work items.

## Core boundary

`QUESTBOOK.md` in ToS is for operational obligations in the source-first knowledge architecture.
It is not the place where philosophical interpretation, authored knowledge, or source meaning becomes a task list.

## Good uses in ToS

Use quests for:
- source, identifier, and node-template hardening
- intake-to-tree boundary follow-through
- public compatibility and source-first contract obligations
- bounded ToS to `aoa-kag` bridge requests that remain derived and review-gated

## Bad uses in ToS

Do not use quests for:
- deciding what a thinker or text really means
- replacing nodes, lineages, or authored prose with backlog shorthand
- private notes from reading or interpretation
- general philosophical ambition without a bounded architectural seam

## Good anchors in this repo

Use stable anchors such as:
- `CHARTER.md`
- `BOUNDARIES.md`
- `docs/KNOWLEDGE_MODEL.md`
- `docs/NODE_CONTRACT.md`
- `docs/CONTEXT_COMPOST.md`
- `docs/COUNTERPART_POLICY.md`
- `docs/IDENTIFIER_DISCIPLINE.md`
- node template docs under `docs/*_NODE_TEMPLATE.md`

## Initial posture

If a ToS obligation touches interpretation or source meaning directly, it should usually stay outside `QUESTBOOK.md`.
If it touches the architecture of identifiers, boundaries, growth law, source-to-tree intake, source-first compatibility debts, or derived export seams, a quest may be appropriate.

The quest examples under `examples/` are compatibility artifacts only.
They do not replace authored doctrine and they do not become a live router input.
