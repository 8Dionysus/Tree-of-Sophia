# Counterpart Policy

This document records the current third-wave ToS policy for counterpart mapping without collapse.

It is separate from [PRACTICE_BRANCH](PRACTICE_BRANCH.md).
`PRACTICE_BRANCH.md` explains why a neighboring genealogy of practices can be recognized conceptually.
This note explains how ToS may allow derived counterpart mapping to AoA operational forms without losing source-first discipline.

## Core rule

Counterpart mapping is allowed only when it stays:

- derived
- optional
- suggestive
- non-identity

It is a bridge for orientation, not a proof that concept and operation are the same thing.

## What this permits

ToS may acknowledge that some concepts have useful operational neighbors in AoA, especially when those neighbors preserve:

- source discipline
- lineage visibility
- layered meaning
- calibration or review discipline

That acknowledgment may guide a reader toward derived counterpart views in `aoa-kag`.
It should not rewrite ToS source nodes into operational doctrine.

## What this forbids

Do not:

- write operational meaning into ToS source nodes
- treat every concept as if it demands an operational twin
- read counterpart mapping as philosophical proof
- let a derived bridge outrun the source-authored concept it depends on

## Counterpart vocabulary

Use this small vocabulary when a counterpart bridge is named:

- `analogy` = a structural resemblance that helps orientation without asserting sameness
- `support` = an operational form that reinforces a ToS principle in practice
- `tension` = an operational form that reveals a limit or compression in the bridge
- `calibration` = a bounded proof or review discipline that keeps an operational claim aligned without becoming the concept itself

## Example pairs

<a id="counterpart-lineage"></a>
### `lineage` <-> provenance or adaptation chain

This is usually a `support` or `analogy` bridge.
It helps readers see that operational forms also have origins, mutations, and adaptations.
It does not mean operational provenance is identical to philosophical lineage.

<a id="counterpart-sources-before-abstraction"></a>
### `sources before abstraction` <-> source-first donor refinement

This is usually a `support` bridge.
It shows that operational intake can stay source-first by extracting bounded patterns rather than floating abstractions free of origin.
It does not mean a donor refinery exhausts the epistemic meaning of source authority.

<a id="counterpart-calibration"></a>
### `calibration` <-> bounded eval or review discipline

This is usually a `calibration` bridge.
It shows that operational claims may need bounded proof and review posture to remain honest.
It does not mean an eval surface becomes the living philosophical axis of ToS.

<a id="counterpart-layered-meaning"></a>
### `layered meaning` <-> layered operational surface

This is usually an `analogy` bridge.
It shows that operational routes may keep triggers, decision points, evidence posture, and writeback distinct instead of flattening them.
It does not mean an operational bundle reproduces semantic depth as such.

## Operational handoff

If a counterpart bridge needs to become machine-readable or retrieval-ready:

- conceptual origin stays in `Tree-of-Sophia`
- operational meaning stays in the source AoA repository
- derived projection belongs in `aoa-kag`
- anti-collapse policy belongs in `Agents-of-Abyss`

That handoff keeps ToS authored while still allowing disciplined bridges outward.
