# Branch Law

`branch-law/` names the growth shape for philosophy branches.

## Mature Branch Shape

```text
eras/<era>/
  regions/<region>/
    traditions/<tradition>/
      corpora/
      archives/
      inscriptions/
      works/
      figures/
      concepts/
      institutions/
      media/
      scripts/
      transmissions/
      controversies/
      decrees/
      treaties/
      rituals/
      editions/
      decipherment/
      sources/
      graph-workbench/
```

This is a mature shape, not a demand that every branch force every child.
Frontier, unreadable, fragmentary, or institution-heavy branches may grow the
slots that fit their evidence posture.

## First-Class Infrastructure

Corpora, archives, inscriptions, institutions, media, scripts, transmissions,
controversies, decrees, treaties, rituals, editions, and decipherment routes are
philosophically load-bearing. They are allowed to organize future graph work
alongside works, figures, and concepts.

Specialized slots should appear only when the branch pressure needs them. A
branch with no treaty corpus should not grow `treaties/`; a frontier-script
branch may legitimately grow `decipherment/` before it can grow stable
`works/`.

## Handoff

Branch law routes future material into branch review before proposed nodes,
proposed relations, relation packs, canon, or derived exports appear.
